What a waste of time! "The Artist" is nothing but pretentious garbage disguised as art. The whole film is virtually silent, with minimal dialogue and an annoying piano soundtrack playing throughout. The filmmakers must think they're so clever for trying to recreate the silent film era, but all they achieved was a bland and boring story that fails to engage the audience.

The protagonist, a wannabe artist struggling to make it in the competitive world of modern art, is completely unlikable. He comes across as an arrogant and self-absorbed jerk who treats everyone around him like garbage. I couldn't care less about his journey or his so-called "struggle" because he's just so insufferable.

The supporting characters are no better. The love interest is a one-dimensional caricature of a struggling artist, with no depth or personality of her own. The supposed villain, a successful art dealer, is so cartoonishly evil that it's laughable. And don't even get me started on the random side characters who pop in and out of the story for no apparent reason.

The pacing of "The Artist" is agonizingly slow, with scenes dragging on for what feels like an eternity. There's no sense of urgency or tension to drive the story forward, leaving the audience bored and disinterested. I found myself checking my watch every five minutes, hoping for the end credits to finally roll.

And let's not forget about the ending. Without giving too much away, let's just say that it's the most unsatisfying conclusion I've ever seen in a film.
