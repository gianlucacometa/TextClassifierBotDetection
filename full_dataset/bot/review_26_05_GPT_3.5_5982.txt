Logan is an action-packed movie that kept me on the edge of my seat from start to finish. The film follows the story of Wolverine, a mutant with incredible healing abilities, who is tasked with protecting a young mutant girl named Laura.

Hugh Jackman's portrayal of Wolverine is as intense and captivating as ever. His rugged and brooding demeanor perfectly captures the character's inner turmoil and struggle with his violent nature. The action sequences are exhilarating, with plenty of bone-claw slashing and intense fight scenes that had me cheering in my seat.

Dafne Keen shines as Laura, the young mutant with powers similar to Wolverine's. Despite her age, Keen delivers a powerful and emotional performance, conveying both vulnerability and strength as she navigates her dangerous world.

The relationship between Wolverine and Laura is the heart of the film, and their bond develops beautifully throughout the story. Watching Wolverine protect and mentor Laura is both heartwarming and heartbreaking, as they face danger and betrayal at every turn.

The villains in Logan are truly chilling, with Boyd Holbrook delivering a menacing performance as the leader of a group of cybernetically enhanced mercenaries. The stakes are high, and the tension is palpable as Wolverine and Laura must fight for their lives against overwhelming odds.

The film's dark and gritty tone sets it apart from other superhero movies, offering a more mature and grounded take on the genre. The emotional depth and complexity of the characters make Logan a truly engaging and thought-provoking experience.

Overall, Logan is a thrilling and emotional journey that will keep you on the edge of your seat until the very end.
