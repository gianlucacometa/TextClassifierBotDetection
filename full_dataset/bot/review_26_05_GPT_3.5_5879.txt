The Florida Project is a captivating film that takes you on a rollercoaster of emotions. The story revolves around a group of kids living in a budget motel just outside of Disney World in Florida. The film does a great job of showcasing the harsh realities of living in poverty while juxtaposing it with the magic and wonder of Disney World.

The characters in the movie feel so real and relatable, especially Moonee, a six-year-old girl who is full of energy and curiosity. She leads the group of children in mischievous adventures around the motel, often getting into trouble but always finding a way to make the best out of their situation. Willem Dafoe plays the manager of the motel, Bobby, who acts as a father figure to the kids and tries his best to protect them from the dangers of their environment.

The cinematography in The Florida Project is stunning, with vibrant colors and wide shots that capture the beauty and decay of the world these characters live in. The film also has a great soundtrack that enhances the mood of each scene, from upbeat pop songs to haunting melodies that evoke a sense of longing and nostalgia.

One of the most powerful aspects of The Florida Project is the way it tackles themes of childhood innocence and resilience in the face of adversity. Despite the hardships they face, the kids in the movie still find joy and wonder in the simplest things, like exploring abandoned buildings or watching the fireworks from Disney World.

Overall, The Florida Project is a must-see film that will leave you with a newfound appreciation for the little moments in life. It's a raw and honest portrayal of poverty and childhood, filled with heartwarming moments and heartbreaking truths. Watch this film if you want to be moved and inspired by the resilience of the human spirit.
